## Tier 1 — Elite / National-International 🌟 {#tier-1}

Activities that signal exceptional achievement beyond school level.

| Category | Examples |
|----------|---------|
| Academic competitions | USAMO, USACO Platinum, IMO, IOI, APMO, Olympic Quốc gia đạt giải |
| Research | Published paper (arXiv, journal), presenter at academic conference |
| Entrepreneurship | Startup with real revenue (>$1,000), app with >10,000 users |
| Sports | National team, international tournament medal |
| Arts | National competition winner, professional debut, record label |
| Leadership | National student government, national NGO founder |

**Key signal**: Impact extends beyond school; external judges/validators (not just school).

---

## Tier 2 — Strong / Regional-National ⭐ {#tier-2}

Activities showing significant commitment, leadership, or regional recognition.

| Category | Examples |
|----------|---------|
| Competitions | Provincial gold/silver, USACO Gold/Silver, national Olympiad participant |
| Leadership | Club president (100+ members), class president, captain of varsity team |
| Community | Organized project with measurable impact (numbers matter: "taught 50 students", "raised $2,000") |
| Internship | Paid internship at recognized company, research assistantship at university |
| Arts | Lead role in regional performance, published creative work |
| Volunteering | Long-term program (6+ months) with leadership role |

**Key signal**: Leadership or external recognition at regional level; measurable outcomes.

---

## Tier 3 — Solid / School-Level ✦ {#tier-3}

Regular participation without notable leadership or external recognition.

| Category | Examples |
|----------|---------|
| Club membership | Member of school club (not officer) |
| Sports | School team member (not varsity/captain) |
| Volunteer | Short-term volunteering, 1–2 month programs |
| Arts | School performance, informal band/group |
| Part-time work | Tutoring peers, family business helper |

**Key signal**: Consistent but without external validation or leadership.

---

## Tier 4 — Weak / One-off · {#tier-4}

Single events, participation without commitment.

| Category | Examples |
|----------|---------|
| One-time events | Single charity run, single hackathon with no result |
| Social media | YouTube/TikTok < 1,000 followers without monetization |
| Casual hobbies | "I like reading", "I play guitar" without any product |

**Key signal**: No demonstrated commitment or external impact.

---

## Automatic Upgrade Rules {#upgrade-rules}

The following conditions bump an activity up one tier automatically:

| Condition | Bump |
|-----------|------|
| Student founded the activity (not just joined) | +1 tier |
| Activity has a quantified impact (numbers, scale) | +0.5 tier (round up) |
| Sustained 2+ years with increasing responsibility | +1 tier |
| National or international recognition of any kind | +1–2 tiers depending on level |

## Major-Specific Weighting {#major-weighting}

Admissions officers weight ECs differently by intended major. When the same activity competes against a major, adjust tier display commentary:

| Major | Highest-value EC types |
|-------|----------------------|
| CS / Engineering | Competitive programming (USACO, VNOI), open-source contribution, hackathon win, robotics |
| Business / Econ | Entrepreneurship, case competition, investment club leadership, Model UN |
| Pre-med / Biology | Hospital shadowing (100+ hrs), clinical research, health NGO, science Olympiad |
| Social Sciences / Policy | Debate, MUN, policy research publication, community organizing |
| Arts / Humanities | Portfolio work, published writing, theatre lead, music performance |
| Math / Pure Science | Math Olympiad, research publication, science fair national level |

Use this table in Gap Analysis to explain *why* a specific activity matters for the student's intended major.
