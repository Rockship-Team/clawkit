# F-1 Visa Interview Preparation Guide

## Overview

F-1 visa interviews are typically 2–5 minutes. The consular officer is assessing:
1. **Intent** — Is the student a genuine student, not an immigrant?
2. **Ability to fund** — Can the family afford the education?
3. **Ties to home country** — Will the student return after graduation?

Most F-1 approvals from Vietnam are straightforward for students admitted to accredited US universities. Preparation reduces nervousness and prevents avoidable refusals.

---

## Most Common Interview Questions (with coaching notes)

### About Your School

**"Why did you choose this university?"**
- Be specific — mention the program, a professor, research opportunity, or co-op program
- ❌ Avoid: "It's a good school" / "It's ranked well"
- ✅ Better: "Georgia Tech's CS program has strong industry ties with companies I want to work with, and I was excited about their research in machine learning."

**"What will you study?"**
- State your major clearly. If undecided, name 1–2 fields of interest.
- Brief follow-up: 1 sentence on why that field.

**"Have you been accepted to other schools?"**
- Answer honestly. Mention your top choice and brief reason.

**"Is this school accredited?"**
- Yes — all US schools that issue I-20s are accredited. You can simply say "Yes, it is."

---

### About Your Plans

**"What do you plan to do after graduation?"**
- Key: emphasize returning to Vietnam or working in a way that contributes to home country.
- ✅ Better: "I plan to return to Vietnam and apply what I've learned to [industry/company/startup here]."
- ❌ Avoid: "I want to stay in the US and work there." (Signals immigrant intent — grounds for refusal)

**"Do you plan to work in the US after graduation?"**
- You can mention OPT (Optional Practical Training — 12 months post-graduation allowed on F-1 visa) but frame it as temporary training before returning home.
- ✅ Better: "I may use OPT to gain 1–2 years of experience, but my long-term plan is to return to Vietnam."

**"Why do you want to study in the US instead of Vietnam?"**
- Specific reasons: access to research facilities, programs not available in Vietnam, international experience, industry connections.

---

### About Finances

**"Who is sponsoring your education?"**
- State clearly: parents, family, scholarship, or combination.
- Be ready to explain the source of funds briefly.

**"How much does this school cost per year?"**
- Know your number (tuition + room + board + fees = ~$X/year).
- Consular officer checks whether the funding is plausible.

**"Do you have a scholarship?"**
- If yes: state the amount and type clearly.
- If no: "My family is sponsoring my education. We have the funds documented."

**"Can your family afford this?"**
- Confident, factual answer: "Yes. My parents have prepared bank statements showing [approximate amount]. The total 4-year cost is approximately $X, and we've planned for this."

---

### About Ties to Vietnam

**"Do you have family in Vietnam?"**
- ✅ Yes — mention parents, siblings, or other close family. This signals ties to home.

**"Do you have property or assets in Vietnam?"**
- If yes, mention briefly — signals rootedness.
- If no, don't volunteer this.

**"Do you have any family or friends in the US?"**
- Answer honestly. Having relatives in the US is not automatically disqualifying, but officers may probe further. Keep the answer simple.

**"Will you return to Vietnam after your studies?"**
- ✅ "Yes. My plan is to return and apply my skills in [industry/field] in Vietnam."

---

### Potential Curveball Questions

**"Your GPA/scores are not very high — why should the US issue you a visa?"**
- Rare, but possible. Focus on your motivation and preparation.
- ✅ "I was admitted by the university's admissions committee based on my overall profile. I'm committed to succeeding academically."

**"Why didn't you apply to universities in other countries?"**
- Frame around the US-specific opportunities for your field of study.

**"What if you fail your studies?"**
- "I have prepared thoroughly. I've also researched the academic support resources at my school and plan to use them."

## Documents to Bring (Checklist)

| Document | Notes |
|----------|-------|
| Passport | Valid ≥ 6 months beyond program start date |
| I-20 | Original, signed by DSO. Check SEVIS ID matches SEVIS fee receipt. |
| DS-160 confirmation page | With barcode |
| SEVIS fee receipt | Printed from fmjfee.com |
| Visa appointment confirmation | Printed |
| Photo | 5×5 cm, white background, taken within 6 months |
| Offer/acceptance letter | From the university |
| Bank statements | Last 3–6 months; shows ≥ 1 year of total cost |
| Financial sponsorship letter | If parents/family are sponsoring — notarized preferred |
| Scholarship letter | If applicable |
| Academic transcripts | High school or current school |
| Diploma / graduation certificate | If graduated |
| Proof of ties to Vietnam | Property documents, family business docs, etc. (optional but helpful) |

---

## Common Refusal Reasons and How to Avoid Them

| Refusal Reason | Prevention |
|----------------|-----------|
| Insufficient ties to home country | Emphasize family, career plan, return intent |
| Inadequate financial evidence | Bank statements must cover full tuition + living costs for at least 1 year |
| Inconsistent answers | Practice key answers; know your school name, cost, and major perfectly |
| Implied intent to immigrate | Never say you want to stay in the US permanently |
| Missing documents | Use the checklist above; bring originals + copies |
| Prior visa violation | Disclose honestly if applicable; prepare explanation |

---

## After the Interview

- **Approved:** Passport kept for 3–7 business days to affix visa. You'll receive a pickup slip.
- **Administrative Processing (AP):** Additional review needed — typically 2–8 weeks. Common for STEM fields. Submit any requested documents promptly.
- **Refused (214b):** Most common refusal code — "failed to establish nonimmigrant intent." You can reapply with stronger evidence of ties to Vietnam.

## Key Phrases to Memorize (English)

- "I have been admitted to [School] for a Bachelor's degree in [Major], starting [Month Year]."
- "My parents are sponsoring my education. We have the necessary funds documented."
- "After graduation, I plan to return to Vietnam and work in [field]."
- "I am eager to gain international experience and bring it back to contribute to [Vietnam/my family/my career]."
