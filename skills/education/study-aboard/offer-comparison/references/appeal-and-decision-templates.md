# Offer Comparison — Templates & Decision Reference

## Financial Aid Appeal Email Templates

### Template 1 — Competing Offer Appeal (most effective)

```
Subject: Financial Aid Appeal — [Full Name], [Student ID], Class of 20XX

Dear [School Name] Financial Aid Office,

I am writing to respectfully request a review of my financial aid package 
for the [Program Name] beginning [Month Year].

I was recently admitted to [Competing School] with a merit scholarship of 
$[X] per year, which would reduce my annual cost to $[net cost]. [School Name] 
remains my first choice due to [specific reason — program quality / research 
opportunity / faculty / culture], and I am eager to join your community.

I am hoping you might be able to review whether additional aid is available 
to help close the gap. I am committed to attending [School Name] if the 
financial situation can be addressed.

I would be happy to provide a copy of the competing offer letter upon request.

Thank you sincerely for your time and consideration.

[Full Name]
[Application ID / Student ID]
[Email] | [Phone]
```

**Khi nào dùng:** Khi em có offer từ ít nhất 1 trường khác với gói tốt hơn.
**Lưu ý:** Gửi đúng địa chỉ Financial Aid Office (không phải Admissions). Attach offer letter từ trường kia nếu được yêu cầu.

### Template 2 — Changed Financial Circumstances Appeal

```
Subject: Financial Aid Reconsideration — [Full Name], [Student ID]

Dear [School Name] Financial Aid Office,

I am writing to request a reconsideration of my financial aid award for 
the [Program Name] starting [Month Year].

Since submitting my application, our family's financial situation has 
changed significantly due to [brief description: e.g., loss of employment, 
medical expenses, change in family income]. Our current financial capacity 
is [describe impact — e.g., reduced by approximately $X annually].

I have enclosed supporting documentation and hope that you will be able 
to review my case. [School Name] is my top choice, and I am deeply committed 
to attending if the financial support makes it feasible.

Thank you for your consideration.

[Full Name]
[Application ID / Student ID]
[Email] | [Phone]
```

**Khi nào dùng:** Khi tình hình tài chính gia đình thay đổi đáng kể sau khi nộp đơn.
**Cần đính kèm:** Tài liệu chứng minh thay đổi (thư thôi việc, giấy tờ y tế, sao kê thuế...).

## Letter of Continued Interest (LOCI) — Waitlist Template

```
Subject: Letter of Continued Interest — [Full Name], [Program], Class of 20XX

Dear [Admissions Committee / specific contact if known],

I am writing to reaffirm that [School Name] remains my first choice, 
and to express my continued strong interest in joining the Class of 20XX.

Since submitting my application, I have [update 1 — e.g., received X award / 
published Y / completed internship at Z / improved SAT score to X]. I wanted 
to share these updates as they reflect my continued growth and commitment 
to [field/values].

I am deeply excited about [specific program, lab, professor, or opportunity 
at this school] and believe I would contribute meaningfully to [specific 
community aspect — e.g., research in Prof. X's lab / the Vietnamese Student 
Association / the undergraduate engineering team].

I remain enthusiastic about the possibility of joining your community and 
would be honored to attend if a space becomes available.

Thank you for your continued consideration.

[Full Name]
[Application ID]
[Email] | [Phone]
```

**Khi nào gửi:** Trong vòng 1–2 tuần sau khi nhận waitlist notification.
**Độ dài:** 250–400 words — không quá dài.
**KHÔNG nên:** Gửi nhiều hơn 1 LOCI, gọi điện liên tục hỏi status.

## Decision Matrix — Enrollment Decision Framework

Use this when the student has 2+ accepted offers and is deciding.

### Step 1: Eliminate Out-of-Budget Options

Net cost = Tuition + Room & Board + Other Fees − Scholarship − Grant (exclude loans)

Flag any school where net cost > annual_budget_usd × 1.2 as ⚠️ financial risk.

### Step 2: Score Remaining Options (1–5 each dimension)

| Dimension | Weight | Scoring Notes |
|-----------|--------|---------------|
| Financial Fit | 35% | 5 = under budget; 3 = ~20% over; 1 = >50% over |
| Program Quality | 25% | Use major-specific ranking (US News by major, not overall) |
| Career Outcomes | 20% | Job placement %, average starting salary, co-op/internship access |
| Location Fit | 10% | Tech hub proximity, cost of living, Vietnamese community presence |
| Campus Culture | 10% | Size (large vs. small), research vs. teaching, diversity |

### Step 3: Calculate Weighted Score

`total_score = sum(score_i × weight_i)`

Present scores transparently — the student makes the final call.

### Step 4: Flag Non-Financial Deal-Breakers

Check:
- Does the school meet the student's major requirements fully?
- Are there any specific resources the student said they want (research lab, specific professor, co-op program)?
- Is there any ED commitment issue? (ED acceptance = binding unless financial hardship)
